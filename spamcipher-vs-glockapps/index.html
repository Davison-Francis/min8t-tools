GlockApps — strengths & limits

The killer feature is the seed-list inbox placement test. You upload your email, GlockApps sends it from your sending infrastructure to its seed addresses across 80+ providers, and reports back: how many landed in inbox, how many in spam/promotions, how many in tabs, how many bounced. That's the closest thing to a real-world deliverability measurement that exists. It's the diagnostic tool when something has gone wrong - your engagement dropped, you migrated infrastructure, you suspect a blocklist hit.

The limits are cost and slow feedback. Each test consumes a quota of sends, costs real time, and isn't free. You're not going to GlockApps-test every draft - it's a final-QA tool. Pricing (as of last public review) starts around $59/month for the entry tier and scales steeply with volume.

MiN8T Spam Score Checker — strengths & limits

MiN8T's tool runs a 21-rule SpamAssassin-style content analysis plus an AI classifier on the message body. It flags trigger phrases ("FREE!!!", "ACT NOW"), excessive capitalization, image-to-text imbalance, missing alt text, suspicious link patterns, broken authentication mentions, and other content-level red flags. It runs instantly in your browser, so you can iterate as fast as you can type.

What it can't do: tell you whether your sender reputation is the actual problem. If your DKIM is misconfigured, your IP is on a blocklist, or your engagement rate has cratered, the rule-based score will look fine but the email will still go to spam at Gmail. Content scoring is a necessary check but not a sufficient one - it's a hygiene gate.

  • Free, instant, unlimited. Run it on every draft.
  • Transparent rule explanations - every flagged issue tells you which rule fired and why.
  • Browser-only for rule analysis; the AI classifier is a stateless API call that doesn't retain content.
  • Complements GlockApps - catch the obvious stuff before you spend a seed-test budget.

Which one fits your workflow?

Use GlockApps when: you're prepping a high-stakes campaign send; engagement has dropped and you need a diagnostic; you've changed sending infrastructure; you're rebuilding sender reputation after a spam-folder slide; you need ongoing inbox-placement monitoring.

Use MiN8T's Spam Score Checker when: you're drafting and want instant feedback on content hygiene; you want to catch trigger phrases before a copywriter ships the draft; you're QA-ing a one-off transactional template; you need a free, no-signup gate everyone on your team can use.

Use both: hygiene-check every draft with MiN8T, then run GlockApps placement testing before launching a campaign. Belt and suspenders.

Frequently asked questions

Is MiN8T's spam checker a replacement for GlockApps?

No. They solve different problems. MiN8T scores message content; GlockApps measures real-world inbox placement across seed-list providers. Pre-send hygiene check vs real-world deliverability test.

When is GlockApps worth the cost?

Before a major campaign launch, when investigating a deliverability drop, after a sending-infrastructure change, or when rebuilding sender reputation. The seed-list placement test diagnoses things content analysis can't - DNS reputation, blocklisting, engagement-based filtering.

How accurate is a rule-based spam score?

It catches obvious failure modes - trigger phrases, broken HTML, missing alt text, suspect links, excessive caps. It doesn't predict per-recipient inbox placement. Use it as a hygiene gate, not a deliverability oracle.

Does MiN8T's tool require signup or send the email anywhere?

No signup. Rule analysis runs in your browser; the AI classifier is a stateless API call that doesn't store content.

Should I use both tools?

Yes - that's the recommended workflow. MiN8T on every draft, GlockApps before major sends.
